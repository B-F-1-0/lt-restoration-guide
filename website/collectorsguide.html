Collector's Guide

You're likely here because you want to collect as much of the Looney Tunes shorts available on home media as of currently. There's a plethora of DVDs, VHSes, and Blu-rays you feel that you should get, but you have no idea which are the best ones without getting duplicates. Lucky for you, you're in the right place.

There has been various sets in varying degrees of availability, but only certain sets are worth having to expand your library to as maximum as possible. So let's get down to business.

Notes

Before we get through with collecting, I have classified a few different tiers of listing the shorts you want to get, being the following:

  • Tier 1: The shorts available restored in HD on Blu-ray, which are in the best quality currently possible. This accounts for 283 Golden Age shorts, plus an additional 50 shorts "unofficially" available, of which 21 of these are also available in SD on DVD.
  • Tier 2: The shorts available restored in SD on DVD, which may not scale well on HD displays. This accounts for 312 Golden Age shorts (of which 21 are available "unofficially" in HD on Blu-ray), plus an additional 18 shorts "unofficially" available.
  • Tier 3: The shorts available but unrestored on DVD or Blu-ray and thus does not necessarily reflect how the cartoon looks on its original theatrical release. This accounts for 109 Golden Age shorts, most scattered on Warner Archive Collection releases as bonus features.
  • Tier 4: The shorts available but unrestored on non-DVD or Blu-ray media. These ones are notoriously a lot more harder to obtain or play, as they're often available only on the out-of-print VHSes or Laserdiscs and often look worse than any of the cartoons in the above tier. This accounts for 122 Golden Age shorts.
  • A total of 129 Golden Age shorts are not officially obtainable by any means on home media.

Depending on your preferences and whether you prefer having shorts in the best available quality, you may only want home media that fits under tier 1 (and if you're fine with SD prints, tier 2). If you're an extreme completionist, you'll want to go for all tiers although it will be overwhelmingly expensive. If you're new, I recommend focusing on tiers 1 and 2 only, as they're a lot more easier to obtain and also look presentable close to how it would be seen in theaters back in the day. With that out of the way, let's get down to business.
